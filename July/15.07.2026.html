1. Global Risk Backdrop & Geopolitics

The war: US and Israel launched their war on Iran on 28 February 2026. A ceasefire/memorandum of understanding held through most of June (Hormuz briefly reopened, oil fell below $70), but broke down in early July. Since then: the US resumed its naval blockade of Iranian ports (14 July), struck targets across Bushehr, Chah Bahar, Jask, Konarak, Abu Musa and Bandar Abbas over multiple consecutive nights, and Iran has retaliated by striking two UAE oil tankers in the Strait of Hormuz (killing one crew member, injuring eight), firing missiles/drones at US infrastructure in Bahrain, and launching missiles that Jordan's air defenses intercepted. Hormuz shipping traffic has collapsed to just 22 ships on 9 July, versus 147 the day before the war began. Trump has floated (then withdrawn) a 20% transit fee on Hormuz shipping, and has warned of strikes on Iranian bridges and power plants "next week" absent a deal.

Market impact: Brent ~$83–85, WTI ~$79–80, both up roughly 10% on the past week alone (and the broader move since the ceasefire collapsed is considerably larger). VIX at 17.16 (+14%), elevated but not yet a full panic regime. Gold holding $4,000–4,140. Equities wobbled mid-week but partly recovered on Tuesday's cooler US CPI print.

Net read: This is a live, active war with a real chance of further escalation flagged explicitly by the US President himself for the coming days — not a contained or fading risk. Position sizing across every instrument in this report should reflect that.
