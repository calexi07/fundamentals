1. USDJPY — Long Highest conviction

Started: Mid-June structural theme, strengthened materially 15-17 July.

Now even more nuanced with the fiscal-credibility angle: JGB yields are rising, which should help JPY, but the driver (Takaichi's fiscal expansion, BoJ-independence fears) undercuts that support — arguably reinforcing rather than weakening the long thesis.

Invalidated if: Fresh intervention (has failed to hold the trend once already); hawkish BoJ surprise; sharp risk-off carry unwind.

2. CADJPY — Long Strengthening

Started: Active since 15 July.

Gets a further boost today: CAD's own central bank just turned more hawkish (Macklem), adding a rate-differential tailwind on top of the existing oil and JPY-weakness tailwinds — now three reinforcing factors instead of two.

Invalidated if: Ceasefire reverses oil; CUSMA resolution removes CAD's trade-policy drag unexpectedly fast; hawkish BoJ surprise.

3. EURUSD — Short Valid, still needs care

Started: Multi-week thesis.

The recent move is explicitly a USD-softness story, not EUR strength — meaning this short is really a bet that USD's own cracks (labor market) don't deepen further, more than a bet on EUR weakness per se.

Invalidated if: Durable ceasefire; sustained close above ~1.1480; continued USD softening without an offsetting EUR-specific negative.

4. CHFJPY — Long CHF Clean haven-vs-funding story

Started: Tracks the broader war timeline; sharpened as JPY weakness accelerated mid-July.

Both currencies are "structurally simple" in opposite directions right now — CHF as the cleanest haven, JPY as the cleanest funding currency — but CHF carries its own dated risk that JPY doesn't.

Invalidated if: The 24 July US-Switzerland tariff deadline resolves badly for CHF; a hawkish BoJ surprise; SNB intervention against franc strength.

5. NZDUSD — Short Risk-off pressure dominates

Started: Ongoing since the war's re-escalation in early July; NZD's 8 July hike only partially offset it.

NZD remains the highest-beta currency in the set — even a genuine, unanimous rate hike hasn't been enough to overcome broad risk-aversion.

Invalidated if: A durable ceasefire triggers a broad risk-on move; RBNZ surprises hawkish at the 2 September review; dairy/China data turns positive.

6. AUDUSD — Short Risk-off plus a new idiosyncratic negative

Started: New emphasis today with the Fortescue/China iron-ore dispute added to the existing risk-off drag.

This is no longer just "AUD vs. risk sentiment" — there's now a genuine, AUD-specific structural story (China actively reducing reliance on Australian iron ore) layered on top.

Invalidated if: The Fortescue/China dispute is resolved or contained; Q2 Australian GDP surprises firm rather than confirming a contraction; RBA delivers a hawkish surprise on 11 August.

7. USDCAD — Mild long USD, lower conviction Narrowing

Started: N/A — this is a narrowing, not a new trade; still nominally USD-favoring but much less clean than a week ago.

CAD's newly hawkish central bank tone (Macklem) is actively working against the USD side of this pair, even as USD's own broader score stays higher for now.

Invalidated if: A concrete CUSMA resolution; a BoC rate hike delivered sooner than the market currently expects; continued US labor-market softening.

Political risk (GBP) vs. a haven currency with almost no policy ammunition (CHF) — either side of this could snap in the other direction on a single headline.

Invalidated if: A market-friendly Chancellor pick calms gilts sharply; the 24 July CHF tariff deadline resolves badly for the franc.

Overall Presumption

With the full set of deep-dives now in hand, the honest read across all eight currencies is that almost none of them offer a clean, single-direction story right now — nearly every one is a tension between a real, identifiable tailwind and an equally real, currency-specific headwind. USDJPY long and CADJPY long remain the cleanest expressions of the current environment precisely because their supporting forces (JPY weakness, Canada's hawkish turn, oil) are currently reinforcing rather than fighting each other. EURUSD short is worth holding but should be understood as a bet on US-specific softness more than European strength. GBP is pure event risk until the Chancellor is named. And CHF and AUD both just picked up genuine, currency-specific catalysts (the 24 July tariff deadline; the Fortescue/China iron-ore dispute) that deserve their own dedicated tracking going forward, separate from the broader war narrative.
